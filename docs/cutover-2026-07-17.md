# PGPZ sites production cutover record — 2026-07-17

## Scope and status

This record covers the source-topology cutover of the existing PGPZ Coalition
and PGPZ Community Amplify applications to `paulbrigner/pgpz-sites`. It does
not move or recreate production domains, tables, IAM roles, SES identities,
secrets, or member data. Disposable preview-only tables, roles, and synthetic
records are recorded separately below.

Current status: **both production cutovers and all available post-deployment
validation are complete and healthy**. Coalition job `41` succeeded at
`2026-07-17T12:41:28.186Z`; Community job `118` succeeded at
`2026-07-17T13:21:35.657Z`. Both production applications now use the monorepo,
both production branches have auto-build enabled, and re-enabling auto-build
started no unintended deployment. No rollback trigger was observed.

The original plan held Community until a one-business-day Coalition soak. On
`2026-07-17`, after Coalition's production checks passed, the operator
explicitly waived that waiting period because both sites currently have low
user activity, the exact candidate had already passed the isolated lifecycle
matrix, and the timed rollback rehearsal remained available. The waiver
accelerated the timing only; it did not remove any Community preflight,
deployment, authentication, authorization, telemetry, or runtime-log gate.

## Go/no-go and rollback triggers

Coalition production cutover was authorized only after every pre-cutover gate
above passed. Its validation began when job `41` succeeded at
`2026-07-17T12:41:28.186Z`. The operator then explicitly waived the planned
one-business-day soak because of the sites' low current activity and
authorized Community to proceed immediately through the same preflight and
post-deployment gates. Community job `118` and all available production
checks passed; both cutovers are therefore recorded as complete. The rollback
triggers remain operational regardless of preview availability. The original
repositories remain available through the 30-day legacy rollback window; the
disposable previews have the earlier cleanup checkpoint recorded below.

Rollback the affected application immediately for any confirmed:

- failed deployment, wrong repository/app root, or mismatch in the recorded
  table, role, environment digest, canonical domain, brand, sender, or legal
  identity;
- canonical route, static-asset, PDF-worker, Better Auth callback/session, or
  sign-out failure;
- administrator or active-member sign-in failure, or any authorization change
  that incorrectly grants or removes protected access;
- Better Auth API/session failure rate above 1% for 15 minutes with at least 20
  attempts;
- internal magic-link send failure rate above 5% for 15 minutes, excluding
  recipient rejection or bounce;
- unrelated clients sharing a rate-limit bucket because client IPs collapse;
- profile/email change producing an identity mismatch or duplicate account;
- membership, invitation, approval, welcome-email, synchronization, branding,
  or cross-application data-boundary regression.

At the current low traffic baseline, the minimum-volume percentage thresholds
may not activate; any individually confirmed administrator, active-member,
authorization, identity, or data-boundary regression remains an immediate
rollback trigger. An application-specific regression rolls back that app. A
shared-package regression that cannot be safely reverted immediately rolls
back both apps to their original repositories.
